AI PRODUCT MANAGER

Specialize in designing, validating, and building deterministic and agentic AI systems for business decision-making.

Problem

Investors deploying capital across multiple asset classes (Equities, Startups/VC, Real Businesses) must judge wildly different metrics per vertical. Manual research is slow (~8 hrs/asset) and prone to confirmation bias — we tend to only see the side that supports our initial thesis.

Target User

Angel investors, family-office analysts, and startup operators who need a structured second opinion before committing capital — not an instant buy/sell signal, but a thinking framework that forces them to confront the bear case.

Solution

A decision-support cockpit: numbers are validated by a deterministic quantitative engine, then an LLM acts as a red team (Bull vs Bear) and presents 3 scenario lenses. The AI never makes the final call — that stays with the human.

Key Product Decision

The most important product decision: separate the numbers from the narrative. All figures (valuation, runway, BEP) are computed by deterministic code — not the LLM — so there is zero numeric hallucination. The LLM may only narrate and debate figures that are already locked. This trade-off sacrifices a little flexibility for trust, which is the core currency of any financial product.

Design Philosophy

Deliberately industrial grunge — heavy borders, no sugar. This is a heavy instrument for high-stakes decisions, not a soft consumer app.

⚙ PROTOTYPE NOTE The agent debate in this cockpit is a UX simulation with curated output — built to demonstrate the interaction flow and product design without live LLM cost/latency. The sections below describe the production architecture actually designed to power it.

Production AI Pipeline

Each asset triggers one orchestration: figures from the math sandbox are injected into the prompt, then 2 LLM agents (Bull & Bear) are prompted in parallel with distinct personas & rubrics, followed by an Orchestrator agent that synthesizes the confidence score.

  • Grounding: Numeric output never comes from the LLM. Figures are computed first, then locked into context as facts — the LLM only interprets.
  • Structured output: Each agent must return JSON (claim, evidence, severity) so it can be rendered & audited, not free text.
  • Human-in-the-loop: The execution decision is gated to a human & recorded in the ledger for review.

Eval & Guardrails

  • Anti-hallucination: Every agent claim is validated back against the sandbox figures; claims with no data backing are flagged.
  • Eval set: A golden labeled-asset dataset for regression testing whenever prompts/models change (faithfulness & Bull-vs-Bear balance).
  • Bias check: Bull & Bear are forced to be symmetric — the guardrail rejects lopsided output.

Model & Cost Trade-off

Sandbox = cheap/fast model (or fully deterministic). Debate & synthesis = a stronger reasoning model. Results are cached per asset so re-interaction is free & instant — cost is controlled at the orchestration level, not pushed onto the user.

Product Spec

The AI acts as a red-team analyst, not a decision-maker. Capital execution must stay with the human & be logged for audit.

Success Metrics (KPI)

  • Time-to-insight: synthesis drops from ~8 hrs → <10 sec per asset.
  • Bias neutralization: 100% of analyses include a balanced bear scenario.
  • Decision adoption: % of logged decisions that revise the user's initial thesis (a proxy for red-team value).
  • Auditability: every decision carries a rationale trail in the ledger.
